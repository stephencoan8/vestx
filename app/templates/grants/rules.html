Understanding how your equity compensation is calculated

üóìÔ∏è Vesting Schedules

RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)

Vesting Frequency: BIANNUAL (Twice per year)

  • Total Duration: Varies by grant type (typically 5-6 years)
  • Vesting Events: Every 6 months on specific dates
  • Typical Dates: May 1 and November 1 (or similar biannual schedule)
  • Cliff: 1 year - nothing vests until first vesting date after 12 months
Example: 1,000 RSU grant on May 1, 2024 (5-year schedule)
  • May 1, 2025: 200 shares (20% - first cliff vest)
  • Nov 1, 2025: 100 shares (10%)
  • May 1, 2026: 100 shares (10%)
  • Nov 1, 2026: 100 shares (10%)
  • ...continues every 6 months through May 1, 2029

ISOs (Incentive Stock Options)

Vesting Frequency: MONTHLY (on the 15th of each month)

Duration and Cliff vary by grant vintage:

5-Year ISO Grants

  • Total Duration: 5 years (60 months)
  • Cliff Period: 1 year
  • Vesting: Starts 1 year after grant, 20% at 6-month cliff (1.5 years total), then monthly on the 15th for remaining 42 months (~1.67% per month)
  • Vest Day: 15th of each month (e.g., 5/15, 6/15, 7/15...)
Example: 1,000 ISO grant on Jan 15, 2024 (5-year)
  • Jan 15, 2025: Vesting begins (1 year after grant)
  • Jul 15, 2025: 200 shares (20% cliff - 6 months of vesting)
  • Aug 15, 2025: ~16.67 shares
  • Sep 15, 2025: ~16.67 shares
  • ...continues monthly on the 15th through Jan 15, 2029

6-Year ISO Grants

  • Total Duration: 6 years (72 months)
  • Cliff Period: 2 years
  • Vesting: Starts 2 years after grant, ~33.3% at 6-month cliff (2.5 years total), then monthly on the 15th for remaining 42 months (~1.39% per month)
  • Vest Day: 15th of each month (e.g., 5/15, 6/15, 7/15...)
Example: 1,000 ISO grant on Jan 15, 2024 (6-year)
  • Jan 15, 2026: Vesting begins (2 years after grant)
  • Jul 15, 2026: 333 shares (~33.3% cliff - 6 months of vesting)
  • Aug 15, 2026: ~13.89 shares
  • Sep 15, 2026: ~13.89 shares
  • ...continues monthly on the 15th through Jan 15, 2030

Performance Bonuses (RSUs)

Vesting Frequency: BIANNUAL (same as standard RSUs)

  • Q1 Bonus: 2 years, vests biannually
  • Q2-Q4 Bonus: 1 year, vests biannually (may have shorter cliff or no cliff)

üí∞ Stock Price & Valuation

Grant Price (Cost Basis)

How it's determined:

  • Price is the Fair Market Value (FMV) on the grant date
  • This is typically the price from the most recent tender offer or 409A valuation
  • This becomes your cost basis for each vest event
  • Used to calculate capital gains when you eventually sell

Vest Date Price

Tax implications:

  • Price on the day shares vest determines ordinary income tax liability
  • For RSUs: You're taxed on (shares vested √ó vest date price)
  • Tax is withheld either via shares or cash
  • This vested price becomes your new cost basis for future capital gains

Current Market Price

For calculations:

  • Latest known tender offer price or secondary market price
  • Used to calculate unrealized gains
  • Used in Finance Deep Dive for tax projections

üßæ Tax Withholding at Vest

RSU Tax Treatment

At vest date:

  • RSUs are taxed as ordinary income (W-2)
  • Tax = (Shares Vested √ó FMV at Vest) √ó Your Tax Rate
  • Typical withholding: ~37% federal + ~10% state (CA) = 47% total

Two ways to pay taxes:

  1. Sell-to-Cover: Company sells enough shares to cover taxes
    • Example: 100 shares vest, 47 shares sold for taxes, you receive 53 shares
  2. Cash-to-Cover: You pay taxes in cash, keep all shares
    • Example: 100 shares vest, you pay $X in cash, keep all 100 shares

Shares Received Calculation

If Sell-to-Cover:

Shares Received = Shares Vested - Shares Sold for Taxes

If Cash-to-Cover:

Shares Received = Shares Vested

üìà Capital Gains (When You Sell)

Short-Term Capital Gains

Holding Period: Less than 1 year from vest date

  • Taxed as ordinary income
  • Added to your W-2 income
  • Tax rate = Your marginal tax bracket (22%-37%+)

Calculation:

Gain = (Sale Price - Vest Date Price) √ó Shares Sold

Long-Term Capital Gains

Holding Period: 1 year or more from vest date

  • Taxed at preferential rates
  • Federal: 0%, 15%, or 20% (based on income)
  • State: Still applies (e.g., CA 9.3%)
  • Much lower than ordinary income rates!

Calculation:

Gain = (Sale Price - Vest Date Price) √ó Shares Sold

Cost Basis Tracking

Important: Each vest event has its own cost basis

  • Cost Basis = FMV on the vest date
  • When selling, you choose which "lots" to sell (FIFO, LIFO, specific identification)
  • Holding period starts on vest date (not grant date!)

üéØ ISO (Incentive Stock Option) Special Rules

Exercise and Vesting

  • ISOs must be exercised (you buy the shares)
  • Exercise Price = FMV at grant date (your cost)
  • Can exercise vested options at any time

Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

At Exercise:

  • AMT may apply on the "spread" (FMV at exercise - Exercise Price)
  • Spread is treated as income for AMT purposes
  • AMT rate: 26-28%
  • Important: You may owe AMT even if you don't sell!

Qualifying Disposition

To get favorable tax treatment, hold:

  • 2 years from grant date, AND
  • 1 year from exercise date

If qualified:

  • Entire gain taxed as long-term capital gains
  • Gain = Sale Price - Exercise Price

If disqualifying (sold too early):

  • Spread at exercise = ordinary income
  • Additional gain = short or long-term capital gains

üíµ Unrealized Gain Calculation

Per Vest Event

Unrealized Gain = (Current Price - Cost Basis per Share) √ó Shares Held

Where:

  • Current Price = Latest market/tender price
  • Cost Basis per Share = FMV at vest date
  • Shares Held = Shares received after tax withholding

Total Unrealized Gain

Total = Sum of all vest event unrealized gains

This represents:

  • Paper gains if you sold all shares today
  • Subject to capital gains tax (short or long-term)
  • Not realized until you actually sell
